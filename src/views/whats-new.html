What's New in {{ YEAR }}

Ready for the {{ YEAR }} season

Pool hours, CNSL team information, and meet schedules have been updated for the {{ YEAR }} outdoor pool season, with links back to published sources when you need to double-check details.

Improved accessibility

Navigation now offers a skip-to-content path and predictable keyboard focus when the menu is open. Pool, team, and meet directories announce when updated information is ready, and reduced-motion preferences are respected.

Find a pool by its features

The Pools page now includes published facility features grouped for accessibility, young swimmers and non-swimmers, sports, water play, and amenities. Select what matters for your visit to narrow the list quickly.

A clearer weekly view of pool schedules

Pool schedule details can now be shown in a weekly calendar view, making public swim hours, practices, special schedules, and closures easier to compare across the week. Choose your preferred view on the Settings page.

Your favorites come first

You can select a favorite pool and favorite swim team in Settings so the information you use most is easier to reach. Your choices stay only in your browser on your device.

Location stays in your control

Distance estimates on the Pools page are now off by default. Enable location awareness in Settings only when you want your browser to share your current location for distance estimates, then optionally sort nearby pools by distance.

More team details

The Teams page includes available team staff and practice information, helping families find the people and schedule details connected with their team.

Install it for convenient access

A compact Phone Install option near the bottom of the home page expands only when you need it, with direct installation on supported Android browsers and clearer steps for iPhone and iPad. After content has loaded, key pool, team, and meet information remains available when your connection is limited or offline.

More dependable offline access

The installed app now validates its offline essentials during each build, preserves a working app shell when optional resources are unavailable during installation, and shows a clear offline page when a page has not been cached yet.

Safer links from published information

Pool, team, and meet pages now validate public website, map, email, and telephone links before presenting them, while safely displaying published directory text.

Anonymous analytics with clearer privacy disclosure

The deployed site uses anonymized aggregate Google Analytics measurement to understand general use without tracking individual visitors. Browser security rules also limit which external services the app may contact.

Weather safety alerts when storms matter

From one hour before today's first published pool activity until the last pool closes, a site-wide banner appears when National Weather Service alerts or the near-term forecast indicate thunderstorms or other hazardous conditions. The banner shows its last update time, links directly to Columbia Association's live pool status directory, can be minimized to a warning indicator, and its refresh frequency can be selected in Settings.

Watching official sources for changes

Published pool, team, and meet sources are monitored during the active season so updates can be reviewed and reflected in the app. The FAQ identifies the official public sources used as the source of truth and reports when accepted seasonal data was last checked; confirm time-sensitive details there before you head out.

Easier to find current information

Search and shared-link previews now distinguish pool, team, meet, and volunteer resource pages more clearly, helping families reach the relevant {{ YEAR }} information faster.
