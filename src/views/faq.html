Frequently Asked Questions

Using the App

What is CNSL?

CNSL stands for Columbia Neighborhood Swim League, a summer recreational swim league in Columbia, MD.

How do I find a pool schedule, team practice, or meet?

Use the Pools page for outdoor pool information, the Teams page for team and practice details, and the Meets page for meet dates and host pools.

Why do meet locations show lane and course measurements?

Known host pools are labeled with their recorded lane count and course measurement, including lane length when available, because lane configuration matters when planning heats in meet-management software, and meter-course results need course context when compared with yard-based times. For competitive-swimming course terminology and rules, see USA Swimming Rules & Policies. Columbia Association and CNSL remain the official sources for local pool and meet information.

Can I install the app and use it offline?

Yes. This site is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that can be added to your home screen. After its resources have loaded, key pool, team, and meet information remains available when your connection is limited or offline.

On iPhone or iPad:

  1. Open this website in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button, then select "Add to Home Screen."
  3. Choose a name and tap "Add."

On Android:

  1. Open this website in Chrome.
  2. Open the menu and select "Add to Home screen" or "Install app."
  3. Choose a name and confirm the installation.

Privacy and Preferences

What data is measured or stored?

This web app does not request names, contact details, account identifiers, or other personally identifiable information. The deployed site uses Google Analytics for aggregate site and feature usage reporting, including reviewed campaign labels for visits opened from the published flyer QR code. Analytics storage is denied so the app does not enable Google Analytics identifiers that associate campaign visits with a visitor across visits; advertising personalization and Google signals are disabled.

App-defined measurement does not send visitor names, contact details, current coordinates, saved settings values, selected pools or teams, selected filters, free-form content, arbitrary URL query or fragment values, referrers, or outbound-link destination details. For an app-published campaign link, it may report its fixed source, medium, and campaign labels for aggregate source attribution after clearing those tags from the visible address. You can also read Google Analytics guidance on personally identifiable information.

Does the app use my location?

Distance estimates are off by default. If you turn location awareness on in Settings, your browser provides your current location to calculate nearby pool distances. App-defined measurement does not send your coordinates.

Where are my preferences saved?

Settings are saved in your browser on your device. Preference changes may be measured only using visible fixed choices or published directory options. You can remove your local choices from the Settings page or by clearing this site's browser data.
