Frequently Asked Questions

What is CNSL?

CNSL stands for Columbia Neighborhood Swim League, a summer recreational swim league in Columbia, MD.

How do I find my team's practice schedule?

Visit the Teams page to find your team's practice schedule and other information.

Where are meets held?

Meets are held at team home pools. Visit the Meets page for details on upcoming meets and locations.

How can my team request a data update?

Team representatives can email simonkurtz@gmail.com with the subject "CNSL Data Update" and the corrected information or published source link.

Is this an official web app?

No. It is not operated by or affiliated with Columbia Association (CA). CA does not provide an API for publicly-available data and directs users to their websites and app. This web app was created by a swim parent, who wanted an easier, quicker, and more tailored path to get pool and team information. All data used for this web app is accessible publicly via CA websites.
This web app provides information for the 2026 outdoor pool season; please verify schedule details with official CA and CNSL sources.

Can I add this to my phone's home screen like an app?

Yes! This website is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that can be added to your home screen for easy access. It is fully functioning without an internet connection as all team, pool, and meet data is part of the web app.

On iPhone/iPad:
1. Open this website in Safari
2. Tap the Share button (square with arrow pointing up)
3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
4. Give it a name (like "CNSL") and tap "Add"

On Android:
1. Open this website in Chrome
2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮)
3. Tap "Add to Home screen" or "Install app"
4. Give it a name and tap "Add"

Once added, you can launch it from your home screen just like any other app! It will work offline for previously viewed content.

What data do you track or keep?

No personally identifiable information (PII) is tracked.
This web app uses a standard Google Analytics tag to help me generally understand usage (page hits, mobile or desktop, etc.). It tracks anonymized behavior (how a user may navigate a page), but it knows nothing about you as an individual. Such use is very common across many websites and apps you may use. The web app is free. Maintaining and hosting it on GitHub Pages is free. I do not intend to monetize with ads, subscriptions, purchases, selling data (which I don't track anyway), or any other means. This web app exists for the community and is a labor of love that exists solely for your and my convenience as we all navigate yet another busy summer.

Settings such as light or dark appearance, favorite team, and favorite pool are different: they are saved only in your browser on your device. They are not uploaded or shared with the site owner. You can remove them at any time from the Settings page or by clearing this site's browser data.

You can also read Google Analytic's take on PII here.
